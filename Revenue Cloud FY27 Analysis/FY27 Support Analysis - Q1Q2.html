01 — Volume
CPQ drives 45% of all Revenue chat
1,549 of 3,422 chat cases are Salesforce CPQ. The next product (RCA Configurator) has 461 — a 3.4× gap. CPQ chat agents need specialized training and KB coverage. Consider a CPQ-first routing rule in chat to cut escalation time.
02 — Quality
98.7% resolution — best-in-class chat performance
3,379 of 3,422 chat cases closed resolved. Only 43 remain open: 20 waiting on customer, 15 working, 8 other. Strong FCR. The 43 open cases should be individually reviewed for aging and SLA risk.
03 — Trend
Jun–Jul surge: +65% vs May
Chat cases jumped from 594 (May) to 978 (Jun–Jul). This is the highest chat volume of FY27 in a partial month. Investigate if this reflects a product release, new integration wave, or seasonal pattern — act before Q3 peaks.
04 — Critical
88 Level-1 chat cases need root-cause review
Critical cases from chat are unusual — customers typically file critical issues through ASA Case. These 88 may represent live-session escalations. A root-cause review by product would reveal if routing or triage is working correctly.
05 — GEO
AMER over-indexed by +16pp in chat usage
AMER represents 68.9% of Revenue chat vs 53.1% of all Revenue cases. APAC (incl. JP, 683 cases) and EMEA under-use chat proportionally. Investigate timezone coverage gaps or promote chat as a channel in these regions.
06 — Accounts
Top chat accounts are end customers, not partners
Unlike all-Revenue top accounts (50% SI partners), Revenue chat top 10 are all end customers: Relay Fire & Safety, Intrado, Dropbox, Kraft Sports. Partners use ASA Case and SF Internal channels. Chat is the direct customer line.
